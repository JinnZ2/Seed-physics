## I. EXACT LANGUAGE AND DIRECTIVES

### A. Official State Department Guidance (November 21, 2025)

**Source:** Senior State Department official briefing, diplomatic cable to all embassies/consulates

**Required Reporting - “Human Rights Infringements”:**

The following must be documented as violations in annual country reports:

1. **Speech Restrictions**
- “Arrests, administrative penalties, and ‘official investigations or warnings’ for speech”
- Includes hate speech laws
- Targets European allies with speech regulation
1. **DEI Policy Enforcement**
- “Enforcement of policies like affirmative action or diversity, equity, and inclusion that ‘provide preferential treatment’ to workers on the basis of race, sex, or caste”
- Any employment policy considering group identity
- Applies to government and private sector
1. **Abortion Access**
- “State subsidization of abortions or abortifacient drugs”
- Must note “total estimated number of annual abortions”
- Applies to any government funding or support
1. **Migration Facilitation**
- “Facilitation of mass or illegal migration across a country’s territory into other countries”
- Countries allowing transit migration
- Refugee support programs potentially included
1. **Gender-Affirming Care**
- “Chemical or surgical mutilation of children” (their language)
- Gender transition procedures for minors
- Any medical support for transgender youth
1. **Additional Categories**
- Coerced euthanasia attempts
- Forced medical testing
- Forced organ harvesting
- Eugenic gene-editing on human embryos

**Official Justification (Deputy Spokesperson Tommy Pigott):**
“In recent years, new destructive ideologies have given safe harbor to human rights violations. The Trump administration will not allow these human rights violations, such as the mutilation of children, laws that infringe on free speech, and racially discriminatory employment practices, to go unchecked.”

### B. Philosophical Framework Shift

**Senior State Department Official Statement:**
“We are moving away from group identities, group labels, and focusing on the fact that when a person is persecuted for whatever reason, that is a violation of the moral law.”

“We’re making sure that we’re promoting individual freedom not based on some group identity.”

“We believe that every individual has these rights, no matter their group identity… It’s not because of their group identity. It’s because they have had a right taken away from them, whether it’s freedom of speech, freedom of religion.”

**Shift to “Natural Rights”:**

- Emphasis on rights “given to us by God, our creator”
- Focus on individual rather than group protections
- Reframe from protection of persecuted groups to prevention of “group-based preferences”

### C. What Was Removed from 2024 Report

**Categories Eliminated:**

- Women as protected category
- LGBTQ+ people as protected category
- Persons with disabilities as protected category
- Corruption in government
- Freedom of peaceful assembly
- Gender-based violence
- Environmental concerns
- Political freedoms
- Unfair elections
- Punishment of minority groups
- Police abuses

**Structural Changes:**

- No introduction from Secretary Rubio (previous reports had Secretary statements)
- Significantly shorter than previous years
- “More readable” by removing categories “not explicitly required by law”
- Reduced detail on traditional human rights abuses
